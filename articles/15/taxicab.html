Ramanujan taxi-cab numbers

October 4, 2015

Hardy’s famous story about Ramanujan goes:

“I remember once going to see him when he was ill at Putney. I had ridden in taxi cab number 1729 and remarked that the number seemed to me rather a dull one, and that I hoped it was not an unfavorable omen. “No,” he replied, “it is a very interesting number; it is the smallest number expressible as the sum of two cubes in two different ways.”

How did Ramanujan know this? Was he right? In this little project we use Racket to examine 1729 and look for other numbers with similar properties: ramanujan-taxi-cab.rkt
